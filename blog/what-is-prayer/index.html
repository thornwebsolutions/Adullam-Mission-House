What Is Prayer?

June 2, 2026 · Based on Psalm 40

Back to Blog

Prayer isn't about following a formula or assuming a specific posture. It arises naturally from our deep need to connect with God—to express gratitude, to reflect on His character, and to bring our struggles honestly before Him. Psalm 40 beautifully models this authentic flow, inviting us into a prayer life that includes praise, meditation, and lament.

Exploring Psalm 40

Psalm 40 can be divided into three parts:

Verses 1–10: Gratitude, filled with personal and communal praise.
Verses 4–5: Reflection on God's unfailing character and faithfulness.
Verses 11–17: Honesty. David openly shares his current difficulties without reservation.

As you read or hear Psalm 40, notice how David begins by recalling God's past faithfulness, pauses to meditate on God's goodness, then honestly brings his present struggles before Him. This honest rhythm reminds us that prayer is not just praise or petition—it includes the fullness of our experiences.

Breaking Down Traditions to Find True Prayer

Many people think prayer must be formal, perfect, or repetitive. But Psalm 40 challenges this notion by showing us that even great psalmists expressed doubt, pain, and lament alongside praise.

True prayer is honesty—speaking to God as our loving Father who desires our whole selves, including our fears and questions. It's this openness that deepens our relationship and invites God's grace into our lives.

The Power of Writing Prayer: Journaling as Dialogue

Journaling creates space to capture prayers fully—our gratitude, meditations, and laments. Writing down prayers helps us slow down and reflect deeply while creating a record of God's faithfulness over time.

By keeping a prayer journal, we allow ourselves to be honest in a safe place, and we gain a tangible way to see God's hand in our journey.

Closing Encouragement

Prayer is our ongoing, honest relationship with God, embracing praise, reflection, and lament. Journaling is a powerful tool to nurture that relationship and help us recognize God's faithfulness over time.

Make time regularly to journal your prayers and emotions, allowing space for God to speak into your heart and for your faith to grow.

May Psalm 40 inspire you to pray honestly and trust deeply in God's enduring love and faithfulness.
